# Data Protection Officer — Designation Letter

> **How to use this template.** Print on the entity's letterhead, fill the
> square-bracketed placeholders, sign both copies (DPO + appointing officer),
> file one copy with the company records and one with the DPO. Submit the
> essentials to the Ghana Data Protection Commission as part of, or alongside,
> your DC/DP registration. Keep a scanned copy in `docs/compliance/` and link
> it from `COMPLIANCE.md`.

## 1. Designation

The Company hereby designates **[FULL NAME]** ("the DPO"), of
**[RESIDENTIAL / PROFESSIONAL ADDRESS]**, holder of
**[DPO CERTIFICATION REFERENCE, e.g. IAPP CIPP/E, IAPP CIPM, ECPC-B DPO,
Ghana DPC-accredited training number]**, as the **Data Protection Officer**
of the Company with effect from **[YYYY-MM-DD]**.

This designation is made under:

- Section 58 of the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843);
- Article 37 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the "GDPR"), where the Company
  processes personal data of EU/EEA data subjects;
- Section 32 of the Nigeria Data Protection Act, 2023, where applicable;
- Section 31 of the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, where applicable.

## 2. Role and tasks

The DPO's tasks include, but are not limited to, those set out in
Article 39 GDPR, namely:

1. Inform and advise the Company and its employees of their data-protection
   obligations.
2. Monitor compliance with applicable data-protection law and with the
   Company's own policies, including the assignment of responsibilities,
   awareness-raising, and training of staff involved in processing.
3. Provide advice where requested regarding the Data Protection Impact
   Assessment and monitor its performance under Art. 35 GDPR.
4. Co-operate with the relevant supervisory authorities — principally the
   Ghana Data Protection Commission, and where applicable the Nigeria DPC,
   Kenya ODPC, EU lead authority, and the California Privacy Protection
   Agency.
5. Act as the point of contact for supervisory authorities and for data
   subjects on all issues related to the processing of their personal data.
6. Maintain the Records of Processing Activities (ROPA) under Art. 30 GDPR,
   NDPA s.29, Ghana DPA principles s.17, and Kenya DPA s.25.

## 3. Independence

The Company shall ensure that the DPO:

1. Is involved, properly and in a timely manner, in all matters that relate
   to the protection of personal data.
2. Has the necessary resources, access to personal data and processing
   operations, and continuing-training budget to perform the tasks above.
3. Is not dismissed or penalised for performing their tasks (GDPR
   Art. 38(3)).
4. Reports directly to the highest management level — in this Company
   that is **[CEO / SOLE DIRECTOR / BOARD]**.
5. Does **not** receive instructions from the controller or processor
   regarding the performance of DPO tasks.

## 4. Conflict of interest

The DPO certifies that they do not hold any other position within the
Company that determines the purposes and means of the processing of
personal data. Where the DPO also holds a different role within the
Company, that other role does not, in practice, lead the Company in
deciding *why* and *how* personal data are processed.

If such a conflict cannot be avoided (e.g. a single-member company where
the DPO is also the sole director), the Company and the DPO will:

1. Record the conflict in writing here in §4.
2. Subject all significant processing decisions to **[EXTERNAL REVIEWER
   NAME OR ROLE]** for independent oversight prior to go-live.
3. Keep a log of such decisions in the Company's compliance register.

## 5. Publication and notification

The DPO's contact details will be:

- Published in the Company's privacy notice at
  **[https://...]/privacy/** under GDPR Art. 13(1)(b).
- Filed with the **Ghana Data Protection Commission** as part of the
  Company's Data Controller / Processor registration.
- Filed with **[other supervisory authority]** where the Company operates
  in that jurisdiction.

## 6. Indemnity and insurance

The Company will maintain Professional Indemnity and Cyber Liability
insurance with minimum coverage of **[AMOUNT]** throughout the DPO's
tenure, and will name the DPO as a covered insured in respect of the DPO
role. This protection survives termination of this designation for the
applicable discovery period.

## 7. Term and termination

This designation is effective from the date above and continues until
terminated in writing. Termination by the Company must not occur because
the DPO performed their tasks under law (Art. 38(3) GDPR). On
termination, the DPO will provide a hand-over memo covering open DPIAs,
DSAR backlog, incidents, ROPA status, and regulator correspondence.

## 8. Continuing professional development

The Company will fund the DPO's continuing professional development at a
minimum of **[GHS AMOUNT]** per year, targeted at:

- Maintenance of current certifications;
- Annual refresh on Ghana DPA, GDPR, NDPA, Kenya DPA developments;
- Incident response and breach-simulation training.
