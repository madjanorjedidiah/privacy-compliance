# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) — template

**Context — please read first.** This project is free, open-source, and
self-hosted. The project maintainers do **not** act as your processor;
they never see your data. You do not need to sign a DPA with the
maintainers.

This template is provided for *your* use as a deploying organisation
when **you** engage sub-processors (hosting providers, email services,
monitoring vendors, etc.) who will in turn process the personal data
held in your workspace. Fill in your own entity as the Controller and
the vendor as the Processor. It covers Ghana DPA 2012, GDPR Art. 28,
NDPA 2023, Kenya DPA 2019, and the CCPA/CPRA service-provider contract
requirements.

**Must be reviewed by qualified counsel before signature.**

## 1. Definitions

Terms in capitals that are not defined here have the meanings given in
the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843), or — where applicable to
the Customer — in the GDPR, NDPA 2023, Kenya DPA 2019, or CCPA/CPRA.

- **"Personal Data"** — any data on which the law applies to the
  Customer's data subjects that the Customer uploads to, or instructs the
  Processor to process through, the Service.
- **"Service"** — the Privacy Compliance Platform provided by the
  Processor at **[https://mydatacompliance.domainname]**.
- **"Sub-processor"** — any third party the Processor engages to process
  Personal Data on behalf of the Customer.

## 2. Roles and scope

2.1 The Customer is the **Controller** of the Personal Data. The
Processor processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from
the Customer, including the instructions set out in this DPA and in the
MSA.

2.2 Details of the processing — subject matter, duration, nature,
purpose, categories of data, categories of subjects — are set out in
**Annex A**.

2.3 The Processor will notify the Customer without undue delay if it
believes a Customer instruction infringes applicable data protection law.

## 3. Processor obligations

3.1 **Security**. The Processor will implement and maintain the
technical and organisational measures described in **Annex B**,
satisfying Art. 32 GDPR, Ghana DPA s.28, NDPA s.39, Kenya DPA s.41, and
California Civil Code §1798.100(e).

3.2 **Confidentiality**. Personnel authorised to process Personal Data
are under a written confidentiality obligation.

3.3 **Sub-processors**. The Processor's current Sub-processors are
listed in **Annex C**. The Processor will:
(a) give the Customer at least **30 days' prior notice** of any new
Sub-processor by publishing an updated Annex C;
(b) impose on every Sub-processor data-protection obligations at least
as strict as those in this DPA, in writing;
(c) remain fully liable for the Sub-processor's acts and omissions.

3.4 **Rights of data subjects**. Taking into account the nature of the
processing, the Processor will assist the Customer by appropriate
technical and organisational measures — including the in-Service export
and erasure tooling — to fulfil the Customer's obligations to respond
to data subject requests under Arts. 15–22 GDPR, Ghana DPA s.32–37,
Kenya DPA s.26, NDPA s.34–39, and CCPA §§1798.100–125.

3.5 **Breach notification**. The Processor will notify the Customer
without undue delay, and in any event within **24 hours**, after becoming
aware of a Personal Data Breach. The notification will include the
information required for the Customer to meet its own 72-hour regulator
notification obligation (Art. 33 GDPR, Ghana DPA s.43, NDPA s.40).

3.6 **DPIA assistance**. The Processor will provide reasonable
assistance with Data Protection Impact Assessments and prior
consultations under Arts. 35–36 GDPR and s.31 Kenya DPA where the
processing is carried out through the Service.

3.7 **Records**. The Processor will maintain written records of the
categories of processing it carries out on behalf of the Customer
(Art. 30(2) GDPR, NDPA s.29).

3.8 **Audit rights**. The Processor will make available to the Customer
information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, and allow
for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the
Customer or an auditor mandated by the Customer. Audits will be conducted
on not less than 30 days' notice, during business hours, not more than
once per 12 months (unless a breach has occurred), and at the Customer's
expense except where the audit reveals material non-compliance.

## 4. International transfers

4.1 The Processor processes Personal Data primarily in **[HOSTING
LOCATION]**.

4.2 Where Personal Data is transferred from the EEA to a country without
an adequacy decision, the transfer is governed by the EU Standard
Contractual Clauses (Commission Decision 2021/914), incorporated by
reference and with the modules, options and annexes selected per
**Annex D**.

4.3 Where Personal Data is transferred outside Ghana to a country
without an adequate level of protection, the transfer is authorised by
**[STANDARD CONTRACTUAL CLAUSES / CUSTOMER CONSENT / ADEQUACY] under
Ghana DPA s.47**. A Transfer Impact Assessment supporting the transfer
is available to the Customer on request.

## 5. Deletion and return of data

Upon termination of the MSA, and at the Customer's election:
(a) the Processor will export the Customer's Personal Data in a
machine-readable format within **30 days**, and
(b) delete all copies of Personal Data within **60 days** thereafter,
unless law requires storage, in which case the Processor will inform the
Customer of that requirement and limit the processing to what is
strictly required.

## 6. Liability

6.1 Each party is liable for damage caused by processing that breaches
its own obligations under applicable data-protection law.

6.2 Subject to Customer's applicable statutory rights, the Processor's
aggregate liability under this DPA is capped at **the fees paid by the
Customer to the Processor in the 12 months immediately preceding the
event giving rise to the claim**.

6.3 No cap applies to (a) personal-injury or death caused by negligence,
(b) fraud, or (c) any other liability that cannot be lawfully excluded.

## 7. Term and precedence

7.1 This DPA takes effect on the Effective Date and continues until
termination of the MSA plus the periods in §5.

7.2 In the event of conflict between this DPA and the MSA, this DPA
prevails in respect of matters regulated by data-protection law.

## 8. Governing law

This DPA is governed by the laws of **[Ghana / Customer's jurisdiction]**
with the courts of **[place]** having non-exclusive jurisdiction, without
prejudice to the data subjects' mandatory rights under their home law.

## Annex B — Technical and organisational measures

- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+/HSTS 1y); database encryption at rest
  at the hosting provider level.
- Multi-tenant data isolation enforced at model, view, and form-dropdown
  layers (tested).
- Role-based access control — Owner, DPO, Compliance Officer, Auditor,
  Viewer — with decorator enforcement on every write path.
- Account lockout (`django-axes`) after 5 failed attempts / 1 hour.
- Rate limiting per client IP, Redis-backed.
- Authentication audit log (`AuthEvent`) retained 12 months.
- Append-only control-status audit log (`ControlStatusChange`).
- Content Security Policy (`default-src 'self'`, pinned CDNs,
  `frame-ancestors 'none'`).
- Evidence-file uploads: extension whitelist + 10 MiB size cap.
- Secure session + CSRF cookies; HttpOnly session; same-origin
  referrer.
- Secrets managed via environment variables, never committed to the
  repo.
- Daily Postgres backups with tested restore.
- Quarterly compliance review and DSAR/incident drills (see
  `docs/qa/QUARTERLY_REVIEW.md`).
- Sub-processor list maintained in Annex C; 30-day prior notice for
  additions.
